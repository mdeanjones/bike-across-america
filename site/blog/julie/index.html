At some point, after becoming an adult when this kind of thing became an actual possibility, I realized I now have a job and responsibilities that don't typically include multiple consecutive months of free time. I realized that the perfect time for a trip like this wouldn't come along on its own, and if I wanted to ever actually do it, I had to actually do it and make the time for it. So here we are! After many months of talking about it (and not nearly as much time preparing for this as you would expect) it's time to go!

Julie's parents, Bruce and Maryanne, at the end of their 1984 cross-country bicycle trip, at the Atlantic ocean

For this ride, I will be riding the bicycle my mother rode cross-country back in 1984 - a 1974 Raleigh Super Course. You might be thinking "oh Julie's mom must be so happy that she's riding her bike!" In which case you would be wrong. She would prefer I ride a "modern bike" that's "safer". But my dad thinks it's a grand idea! And since he's the weird bicycle guy in an objectively weird bicycle family, that is a good enough endorsement for me.

The 1974 Raleigh Super Course Julie is riding on the trip. Shown here during it's first cross-country journey, in 1984 when Julie's mom rode it.

I can't imagine talking about my love of bicycling without mentioning the bike I won't be riding: my beloved Brompton. I could wax poetic about the beauty of Brompton bicycles endlessly. But I won't. Instead, I'll just say this: when you have a bicycle that you can take anywhere, your whole world opens up in beautiful and unexpected ways.
